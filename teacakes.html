Teacakes
Ingredients

Shopping list

  • 500g Strong white flour plus extra for dusting
  • 10g Salt
  • 10g fast acting yeast
  • 60g Caster Sugar
  • 1 Tsp Cinnamon
  • 50g unsalted butter, softened
  • 300ml cool water
  • veg oil for kneeding
  • 100g Sultanas
  • 100g chopped mixed peel
  • 1 Egg beaten, for glaze

    • Method
    1. Top the flour into a large mixing bowl and add the salt, sugar and cinnamon to one side of the bowl and the yeast to the other, add the butter and 3/4 of the water and turn the mixture round with your fingers, continue to add the water a little at a time until you have picked up all the flour from the sides of the bowl, you may not need all the water or you may need a little more, you want a dough that is soft but not soggy, use the mixture to clean the inside of the bowl keep going until the mixture forms a rough dough
    2. Coat the worksurface with a little veg oil, then tip out the dough and begin to knead, keep kneading for 5-10 mins, work through the initial wet stage, until the dough starts to form a soft smooth skin
    3. When your dough feels smooth and silky, put it into a large lightly oiled bowl, cover with a tea towel and leave to rise until doubled in size, at least one hour but fine to leave it for 2 or even 3 hours
    4. Line 2 backing trays with baking parchment or silicon paper
    5. Tip the sultanas and mixed peel on top of the risen dough in the bowl and start working them into it, after a minute or two, tip the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until the fruit is mixed in
    6. Divide the dough into 8 pieces, shape each into a ball, then use a rolling pin to flatten out each one into a round bun, about 1cm thick, brush the teacakes with beaten egg, transfer them to prepared baking trays spacing them apart
    7. Put each tray inside a clean plastic bag and leave to raise for about an hour until the teacakes have doubled in size, mean while heat the oven to 200 C
    8. Bake the teacakes for 10-15 mins until risen and golden, cool on wire rack
