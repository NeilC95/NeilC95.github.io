Ingredients

    Tyga Pots

    • Ginger and Garlic Paste
    • Tyga Masala No.22
    • Tyga Masala No.2
    • Methi / Fenugreek Sachet

    Shopping List

  • 4 Onions
  • 1 medium potato
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 700ml Vegtable Oil for deep frying
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 100g gram flour
  • coriander

Prep
  1. Finely slice the onions
  2. peel and grate the potato
  3. roughly chop the coriander
Method
  1. In a large bowl add the ginger and garlic paste, Tyga masala no. 2, Tyga masala no. 22, potato, onions, methi, salt and a handful of corander, stir to combine, making sure the onion slices and grated potato are all covered
  2. Stir the egg yolk into the mixture, then add the gram flour, a little at a time, stirring carefully to ensure that there are no lumps, until the mixture starts to stick together
  3. heat the veg oil (enough oil to semi submerge walnut / golfball size bhaji) in a deep sided pan or deep fat fryer, adding a tablespoon of the bhaji mixture whilst you do this, when the mixture starts to rapidly fry, the oil is at the correct temporature
  4. keep a bowl of cold water by the bhaji mixture so that you can keep your hands moist and not sticky with your hands form a loosly shaped walnut size ball of the bhaji mixrture and depending on size of the pan place 4 balls into the oil and cook for 5-6 minutes turning every minute until they are evenly brown and crisp
  5. remove and drain on kitchen paper, repeat with the remaining bhaji mixture
