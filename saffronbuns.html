Saffron Buns
Ingredients

Shopping list

  • 300ml whole milk
  • 1/2 pack saffron
  • 75g butter
  • 500g strong white bread flour
  • 100g golden caster sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten plus extra for egg wash
  • 24 currants

    • Method
    1. Put the milk in to a pan and gently heat until it is steaming, Use pestle and mortar to grind the saffron into a powder. Add this to the milk along with the butter, then set aside until luke warm
    2. In a large bowl mix the flour, caster sugar, 1tsp salt, and the yeast and make a well in the centre, Pour in the milk mixture along with the egg, Mix well to form a sticky dough, and turn out into a work surface and knead until smooth and elastic. Put the dough into a lightly oiled bowl and cover with cling film Set the bowl in a warm part of the house and allow to rise for about 1 hour, until doubled in size
    3. Knock back the dough and devide into 12 equal portions, cover the pieces with the oiled cling film while you make the rolls - this will stop the dough from drying out. working with one piece at a time roll out into a 30cm long strand, roll up one end into the middle and the other end the other way to produce an "S" shape, place the buns on a large or 2 smaller baking trays, lightly cover with oiled cling film and prove until nearly doubled in size
    4. pre-heat overn to 200 deg C, or 180 fan
    5. When ready to cook brush the buns with beaten egg and press one current in the centre of each spiral, place the trays in the oven and cook for around 15 mins, allow to cool before eating
