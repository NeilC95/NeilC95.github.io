Mushroom Bourguignon
Ingredients

  • 4 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 0.50 tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp garlic paste
  • 200 g button mushrooms, halved
  • 200 g chestnut mushrooms, quartered
  • 200 g mini portobello mushrooms, sliced
  • 20 Chantenay carrots, topped, tailed and halved if large
  • 150 g cooked chestnuts, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp concentrated tomato paste
  • 2 Tbsp Henderson's Relish or Tamari (substitute with soy sauce)
  • 240 ml Artesano de Argento Malbec Cabernet Franc red wine
  • 480 ml vegetable stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 1 tsp cornflour
  • 3 Tbsp flat leaf parsley, chopped
  • salt and pepper to season, to taste

Method
  1. Heat the oil in a large lidded pot (Dutch Oven), add the onions, salt and pepper. Pan fry the onions over low heat, stirring, until softened, 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and mushrooms – it will look like A LOT of mushrooms but they will quickly cook down. Sauté for a couple of minutes, stirring.
  3. Add the carrots, chestnuts, tomato paste, Henderson’s Relish, wine and stock. Bring to a boil and add the bay leaf and thyme.
  4. Partially cover the pot, lower the heat and simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has reduced and the carrots are fork tender. Stir in a little hot water if the pot looks like it is getting too dry.
  5. If you feel the sauce needs to be thickened, stir in the cornflour (mixed with a little water) and simmer until the gravy thickens slightly.
  6. Discard the bay leaf and thyme sprig. Check the seasoning, adding salt and pepper to your taste. Stir in half the parsley, reserving some to serve with the dish.
  7. Serve over mashed potatoes with some steamed green vegetables if you like.
