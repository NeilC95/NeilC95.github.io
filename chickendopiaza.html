Ingredients

    Tyga Pots

    • Ginger and Garlic Paste
    • Tyga Masala No.5
    • Tyga Masala No.2
    • Tyga Masala No.14
    • Methi / Fenugreek Sachet

    Shopping List

  • 3 Onions
  • 1 Green Bell Pepper
  • 1 Bunch Coriander (optional)
  • 3 tsp Vegtable Oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp tomato puree
  • 500g Diced Chicken
  • 300g Rice

Prep
  1. Finely dice 1 and 1/2 onions
  2. Cut the other 1 and 1/2 onion into 8ths (wedges)
  3. slice the pepper into similar size wedges as the onion
  4. Finely chop coriander leaves and stalks keeping them seperate
  5. If you dislike while spices, grind the 5 x clove and 5 x cardamom from the Tyga Masala No. 5 in and pestle and mortar
Method
  1. Heat oil in deep frying pan over a high heat and add the diced onion, ginger and garlic paste, Tyga Masala No. 5 (ground or whole 5 x clove and 5 x cardamom), Methi Sachet, coriander stalks and salt. Fry for 4 minutes, stirring continuously.
  2. Transfer to medium heat and add Tyga Masala No. 2, Tyga Masala No. 14 (1/2 tsp for mild, 1 tsp for medium or 2 tsp fot hot), tomato puree, onion wedges and pepper. Fry for a further 2 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add 200ml water and simmer for 4 minutes.
  4. Add the chicken, cover with lid and simmer for a further 15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the chicken is cooked
  5. Garnish with coriander leaves(if using)
Rice
  • Add 1/2 pint of rice to 1/2 pint of cold water, cover and bring to the boil and simmer, still covered for 3 minutes, remove from the heat and leave covered for 45 minuted for the perfect rice
Serve
  • Serve with rice and Mushroom Bhaji
Options
  • For Vegetarian at step 4. add butternut squash and black beans in place of chicken, you may need to reduce the cooking time slightly until butternut squash is cooked
