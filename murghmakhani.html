Ingredients

    Tyga Pots

    • 2 x Ginger and Garlic Paste
    • Tyga Masala No.14
    • Tyga Masala No.21
    • Tyga Masala No.2
    • Methi leaves Sachet
    • Coconut Cream Powder

    Shopping List

  • 2 Large Onions
  • 2 tbsp natural yoghurt
  • 5 tomatoes (medium)
  • 3 tsp Vegtable Oil
  • 150ml single cream
  • 25g butter
  • 600g Diced Chicken
  • coriander (optional)

Prep
  1. Finely the onions
  2. In a large bowl add the chicken, ginger and garlic paste Tyga masala no. 21, 1/2 tsp salt, 1tbsp oil, and 2tbsp yoghurt, mix well and marinade for anything from 30 minutes to 24 hours (the longer the better)
  3. chop the tomatoes into wedges
  4. Finely chop coriander if using
Method
  1. once marinaded bake the chicken on a griddle or baking tray in an oven at 200 deg C for 10 mins turning half way through cooking once cooked set aside to add later
  2. Meanwhile in a large deep frying pan with a lid, add 2tbsp oil onions, garlic and ginger paste, methi leaves and 1tsp salt fry for 2 mins stirring continuously
  3. Add Tyga masala no. 2, Tyga masala no. 14 (1 tsp for mild, 2 tsp for medium or 3 tsp for hot) and coconut powder, mix in well and simmer for 2 mins, add 50ml water and simmer for further 2 minutes (option for smoother sauce, blend with hand blender)
  4. Remove the chicken from the griddle or baking trayleaving behind any juices, add the chicken to the frying pan and add 150ml cream, the butter and simmer uncovered for 5 minutes or until the chicken is cooked
  5. Garnish with coriander leaves(if using)
Options
  • For Vegetarian alternate try using green peppers or green beans instead of chicken For a meat alternate replace the chicken with fish, either cod or pollock
