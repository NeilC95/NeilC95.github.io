Ingredients

    Tyga Pots

    • Ginger and Garlic Paste
    • Tyga Masala No.22
    • Tyga Masala No.27
    • Coconut Powder
    • 2 x Curry Leaves Sachet

    Shopping List

  • 2 Onions
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 1 Bunch Coriander (optional)
  • 3 tsp Vegtable Oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 600g Diced Chicken
  • 400ml Coconut Milk
  • 4 Naan Breads

Prep
  1. Finely dice the onions
  2. Roughly chop the tomatoes
  3. Chop the coriander discarding the stems
Method
  1. Heat oil in deep frying pan over a high heat and add the onion, ginger and garlic paste, Tyga Masala No. 22, salt and Curry leaves. cook for 4 minutes, stirring continuously.
  2. Transfer to medium heat and add the tomatoes, cover with lid and cook for a further 5 minutes, stirring occasionally
  3. Add Tyge Masala No. 27 (1/2 the pot for medium and the whole pot for hot) and simmer for 2 minutes, add 100ml water and bring to the boil
  4. Add the chicken, stir well, cover with lid and cook on a medium heat, stirring occasionally, or until chicken is cooked through.
  5. Stir in the Coconut Powder and the coconut milk, cook uncovered for a further 10 minutes until the sauce is a thick consistancy
  6. Sprinkle with coriander (if using)
Serve
  • Serve with Naan Bread
Options
  • For Vegetarian at step 4. add butternut squash and green beans in place of chicken, and step 5. you may need to reduce the cooking time slightly until the squash is cooked and sauce is warmed through
  • To make with white-flesh fish or prawns, at step 4. add cod or pollock fillets, or prawns, in place of chicken and cook for 5 minutes, at step 5. you may need to reduce cooking time otherwise the fish will be overcooked, Ensure the sauce is thick and warmed through
