Chelsea Buns
Ingredients

Shopping list

  • 500g Strong white flour plus extra for dusting
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 7g fast acting yeast
  • 300ml Milk
  • 40g butter at room temperature
  • 1 egg
  • Vegetable oil for greasing

    • For the filling

  • 25g butter melted
  • 75g soft brown sugar
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 150g dried mixed fruit

    • For the glaze

  • 2 tbsp milk
  • 2 tbsp caster sugar

    • Method
    1. sift the flour and salt into a large bowl. Make a well in the middle and add the yeast
    2. Meanwhile ward the milk and butter in a saucepan until the butter melts and the mixture is lukewarm
    3. Add the milk mixture and the Egg to the flour mixture and stir until the contentsof the bowl come to gether in a soft dough(you may need a a little more flour)
    4. Tip the mixture onto a generously floured work surfase. Knead for 5 minutes, adding more flour if necesary, until the dough is smooth and elastic and no longer feels sticky
    5. Lightly oil a bown and pleace the dough in the bowl and turn until it is covered in oil, Cover the bowl with cling filk and set aside in a warm place for one hour, or until the dough has doubled in size
    6. Lightly grease a baking tray
    7. For the filling, knock the dough back to its original size and turn out into a lightly floured work surface, roll the dough out into a rectangle about 5mm thick. Brush all over with the melted butter, then sprinkle with the brown sugar, cinnamon and dried fruit
    8. Roll up the dough into a tight cylinder, but tem 4cm slices and place them onto ligthly greased baking sheet, leaving a little space between each slice, cover with tea towel and set aside to rise for 30 mins
    9. pre-heat oven to 190 deg C
    10. Bake the buns in the over for 20-25 mins, or until risen and golden-brown
    11. Meanwhile for the glaze, heat the milk and sugar in a saucepan until boiling, reduce the heat and allow them to simmer for 2-3 mins
    12. Remove the buns from the oven and brush with the glaze, then set aside to cool on a wire rack
